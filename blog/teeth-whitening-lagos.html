Teeth Whitening in Lagos: What to Expect, Cost & Results

Cosmetic Dentistry · January 15, 2026 · 6 min read

A bright, white smile is one of the most noticeable improvements you can make to your appearance — and professional teeth whitening in Lagos delivers faster, safer, and more dramatic results than any over-the-counter product. This guide covers everything Ikeja and Lagos residents need to know before booking their first whitening session.

Why Do Teeth Stain in the First Place?

Tooth discolouration falls into two categories: extrinsic staining (on the tooth surface) and intrinsic staining (within the tooth structure). The most common culprits in Lagos include:

  • Daily tea and coffee consumption
  • Red palm oil and heavily pigmented foods
  • Tobacco use (smoking or chewing)
  • Fluoride exposure during tooth development
  • Natural ageing — enamel thins, revealing the yellowish dentine beneath
  • Certain antibiotics (tetracycline) taken during childhood

Professional whitening is most effective on extrinsic stains and age-related yellowing. Intrinsic staining from antibiotics or trauma may need additional treatment such as veneers, which our cosmetic team can also advise on.

Professional Whitening vs DIY Kits: What's the Difference?

Supermarket whitening strips and toothpastes contain very low concentrations of whitening agents — typically 0.1% hydrogen peroxide, the legal limit for over-the-counter products in Nigeria. They may produce a subtle improvement over weeks of daily use, but the results are rarely dramatic and often uneven.

In-clinic whitening at PHIL Dental Clinic uses a prescription-strength peroxide gel applied precisely to your teeth by a trained clinician. The higher concentration, combined with professional application technique, means:

  • Results of up to 8 shades whiter in a single session
  • Even coverage across all visible teeth
  • The process is monitored to protect your gums and enamel
  • Results that last 12–24 months with basic maintenance

At PHIL Dental Clinic in Ikeja, we use a professional-grade whitening system that delivers clinically measurable results in one visit — with no damage to enamel when performed by our qualified team.

What Happens During a Whitening Session?

The entire appointment takes approximately 60–90 minutes. Here is what to expect step by step:

  1. Initial assessment: We check your teeth and gums to confirm they are healthy enough for whitening. Active decay or gum disease must be treated first.
  2. Shade recording: We take a baseline shade reading so you can see the measurable improvement after treatment.
  3. Gum protection: A barrier gel is applied to shield your gums from the whitening agent.
  4. Gel application: Whitening gel is applied to each tooth and left to work for the recommended exposure time.
  5. Final shade check: We compare your post-treatment shade against the baseline — this is the satisfying part.
  6. Take-home kit (optional): We can provide custom-fitted trays and maintenance gel so you can top up results at home.

How Much Does Teeth Whitening Cost in Lagos?

Prices vary significantly across Lagos depending on the type of treatment, the clinic's equipment, and the expertise of the practitioner. As a general guide in 2026:

  • OTC whitening strips: ₦2,000 – ₦8,000 (minimal results)
  • Take-home dentist kit only: ₦15,000 – ₦35,000
  • In-clinic professional whitening: ₦40,000 – ₦120,000 depending on the system used

At PHIL Dental Clinic, we provide a clear, all-inclusive price after your free consultation. We do not charge separately for the shade assessment, gum protection, or aftercare advice.

How Long Do Results Last?

Professional whitening results typically last 12 to 24 months. Longevity depends on your diet and lifestyle — patients who drink black coffee daily or smoke will see faster re-staining. The take-home maintenance kit we provide allows you to do short top-up sessions every few months to keep results fresh without returning to the clinic.

Is Teeth Whitening Safe?

Yes — when performed by a qualified dental professional using properly formulated products, whitening is very safe. Some patients experience mild tooth sensitivity for 24–48 hours after treatment, which subsides on its own. We use a desensitising agent as part of our protocol to minimise this.

Whitening is not recommended during pregnancy, for patients with active decay or severe gum disease, or for children under 18. We assess all of these factors at your consultation before beginning any treatment.
