Dental Implants in Lagos: Everything You Need to Know Before You Decide

Restorative Dentistry · February 10, 2026 · 8 min read

Losing a tooth — whether to decay, injury, or gum disease — affects far more than appearance. It changes how you bite, how you speak, and over time it causes the surrounding bone to shrink. Dental implants are the only tooth replacement option that addresses all of these problems at once. This guide explains exactly how they work, what the process looks like in Lagos, and what you should expect to pay in 2026.

What Is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a small titanium screw surgically placed into the jawbone at the site of the missing tooth. Over 3–6 months, the bone fuses with the titanium through a process called osseointegration — essentially growing around the implant to hold it permanently in place. Once integrated, a ceramic crown is attached on top, creating a result that looks, feels, and functions exactly like a natural tooth.

Unlike dentures (which rest on the gum surface) or bridges (which grind down adjacent healthy teeth), implants are freestanding. They preserve the bone, require no modification to neighbouring teeth, and can last a lifetime.

Am I a Good Candidate for Implants?

Most healthy adults with a missing tooth are candidates. A successful implant requires:

  • Adequate jawbone density — enough bone to anchor the implant. If bone has been lost, a bone graft may be needed first.
  • Healthy gums — active gum disease must be treated before implant placement.
  • Good general health — uncontrolled diabetes or certain medications can affect healing.
  • Non-smoker or willing to quit — smoking significantly increases implant failure rates.

We assess all of these factors with X-rays and a clinical examination at your consultation before recommending implants.

The Step-by-Step Implant Process at PHIL Dental Clinic

Stage 1: Consultation and Planning

We take digital X-rays to assess bone volume, examine the surrounding teeth, and create a precise treatment plan. You receive a full cost breakdown at this stage — no surprises later.

Stage 2: Implant Placement

Under local anaesthesia, the titanium implant is placed into the jawbone. The procedure is typically completed in 45–90 minutes. Most patients are surprised at how little discomfort they feel during the procedure, and mild soreness over the following 2–3 days is easily managed with over-the-counter pain relief.

Stage 3: Healing (Osseointegration)

The implant is left to integrate with the bone over 3–6 months. During this period, you continue eating and living normally — we may place a temporary tooth for aesthetics if the gap is visible.

Stage 4: Crown Fitting

Once the implant is stable, we attach a custom-made ceramic crown matched precisely to the colour, size, and shape of your natural teeth. This is your final, permanent tooth.

From placement to final crown, the full implant process typically takes 4–7 months at PHIL Dental Clinic. We manage every stage in-house — no referrals to separate specialists.

How Much Do Dental Implants Cost in Lagos?

Implant pricing in Nigeria varies considerably depending on the quality of the implant system used, the clinic's equipment, and the surgeon's experience. Approximate 2026 ranges:

  • Single implant (implant + crown): ₦350,000 – ₦800,000
  • Implant with bone graft: add ₦80,000 – ₦200,000
  • Multiple implants: volume discounts usually apply

At PHIL Dental Clinic, we use internationally certified implant systems. We provide a complete written cost estimate at consultation so you can make an informed decision with no hidden fees.

How Long Do Dental Implants Last?

With proper care, a dental implant can last 20–30 years or longer — effectively a lifetime for most patients. The crown on top may need replacing after 15–20 years due to normal wear. Maintaining good oral hygiene and attending regular check-ups are the two most important factors in implant longevity.
