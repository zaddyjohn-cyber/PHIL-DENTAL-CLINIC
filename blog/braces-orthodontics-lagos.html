Braces or Clear Aligners? Your Complete Guide to Orthodontics in Lagos

Orthodontics · June 3, 2026 · 7 min read

Crooked or crowded teeth are not just a cosmetic issue — they affect how you bite, how you clean your teeth, and long-term can contribute to jaw problems and accelerated wear. Orthodontic treatment in Lagos has advanced significantly, and patients now have two main options: traditional metal braces or modern clear aligners. This guide helps you decide which is right for your situation and budget.

How Do Braces and Aligners Actually Work?

Both systems work by applying consistent, gentle pressure to teeth to move them into the correct position over time. The difference is in the mechanism:

  • Traditional braces use metal brackets bonded to each tooth, connected by an archwire that is adjusted periodically by your orthodontist. The brackets and wire are fixed in place — you cannot remove them.
  • Clear aligners are a series of custom-made, transparent plastic trays. Each tray is slightly different from the last, progressively moving teeth toward the target alignment. You wear each tray for 1–2 weeks before moving to the next. They are removable for eating and brushing.

Traditional Metal Braces: Pros and Cons

Advantages

  • More powerful for complex corrections — severely crowded teeth, significant bite problems, or large gaps
  • No patient compliance required — they are always working
  • Generally lower cost in Nigeria
  • Highly effective for growing children and teenagers
  • Treatment progress is fully controlled by your orthodontist

Considerations

  • Visible — though tooth-coloured ceramic brackets are available as a less noticeable alternative
  • More dietary restrictions (avoid hard, sticky foods)
  • Can cause minor discomfort for 2–3 days after each adjustment
  • Cleaning around brackets requires extra effort

Clear Aligners: Pros and Cons

Advantages

  • Nearly invisible — most people will not notice you are wearing them
  • Removable — eat whatever you like, brush normally
  • Smoother and generally more comfortable than metal brackets
  • Fewer clinic visits for adjustments
  • Good for mild to moderate crowding, spacing, and certain bite corrections

Considerations

  • Must be worn 20–22 hours per day to be effective — discipline is required
  • Not suitable for the most severe orthodontic cases
  • Generally higher cost than traditional braces
  • Trays must be removed before eating or drinking anything except water

Cost Comparison in Lagos (2026)

Orthodontic pricing in Lagos varies depending on treatment duration, case complexity, and the materials used:

  • Metal braces: ₦150,000 – ₦400,000 for a full course of treatment
  • Ceramic (tooth-coloured) braces: ₦200,000 – ₦500,000
  • Clear aligners: ₦350,000 – ₦700,000 depending on complexity

These costs typically include all adjustment appointments throughout treatment and final retainers. At PHIL Dental Clinic, we provide a complete, written cost plan at the start of treatment so you know the total investment upfront.

How Long Does Orthodontic Treatment Take?

Treatment duration depends heavily on case complexity:

  • Minor crowding or spacing: 6–12 months
  • Moderate cases: 12–18 months
  • Severe crowding or bite correction: 18–30 months

Clear aligners can sometimes achieve results faster than braces for straightforward cases, but for complex bite corrections, braces typically provide more predictable outcomes.

At What Age Can You Start Orthodontic Treatment?

Orthodontic treatment is most efficient in children and teenagers when the jaw is still developing — typically ages 10–17. However, we treat adult patients successfully at all ages. There is no upper age limit for orthodontics; the teeth respond to treatment regardless of age, just sometimes a little more slowly in adults.
