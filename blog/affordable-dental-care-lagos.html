Affordable Dental Care in Lagos — Without Compromising Quality

Patient Guide · May 8, 2026 · 5 min read

The words "affordable" and "quality" often seem mutually exclusive when it comes to dental care in Lagos. But the truth is that the most cost-effective dental strategy is not finding the cheapest provider — it is preventing expensive problems in the first place. This guide explains how to keep your dental costs genuinely low without cutting corners on care.

Why Prevention Is the Most Affordable Dental Strategy

The single most effective way to reduce dental costs is to attend regular check-ups. A routine cleaning and examination at PHIL Dental Clinic costs a fraction of what you would spend treating a cavity that was left to become a root canal, or gum disease that was ignored until extractions were needed.

Consider the cost comparison:

  • Routine scaling and polishing: ₦8,000 – ₦20,000 every 6 months
  • Composite filling (early decay): ₦15,000 – ₦35,000
  • Root canal (decay that was not treated): ₦60,000 – ₦150,000
  • Dental implant (after extraction): ₦350,000 – ₦800,000

Each step on that list represents what happens when the previous intervention was skipped. The maths strongly favours prevention.

What "Affordable" Should and Shouldn't Mean

Affordable dental care means fair, transparent pricing for quality treatment — not discounted care that cuts corners on materials, sterilisation, or clinician training. In Lagos, it is unfortunately common to find clinics charging very low rates by using substandard filling materials, skipping proper sterilisation steps, or performing procedures they are not qualified for. The result is treatment that fails early, causes complications, and ultimately costs you far more to correct.

At PHIL Dental Clinic, we use internationally certified materials and equipment across all treatments. Our pricing is transparent — you receive a full written estimate before any treatment begins, with no hidden charges.

How to Keep Your Dental Bills Low at PHIL Dental Clinic

Book before pain starts

Come in for a check-up every 6 months even when nothing hurts. We identify problems at their smallest — when they are cheapest to treat.

Ask for a full treatment plan

If multiple treatments are recommended, ask us to prioritise them by urgency. This lets you spread costs over time without jeopardising your oral health.

Use scaling and polishing as your baseline

Professional cleaning twice a year removes the plaque and tartar that cause both decay and gum disease. It is one of the most cost-effective dental investments you can make.

Address sensitivity early

Tooth sensitivity is often an early warning sign. Treating sensitivity costs a fraction of what a root canal costs once infection reaches the nerve.

What to Watch Out for in "Cheap" Dental Clinics

When a dental price seems too good to be true in Lagos, ask yourself:

  • Are instruments coming from individually sealed, sterilised packs?
  • Is the clinician registered with the MDCN?
  • Are you given a written quote before treatment starts?
  • Is the clinic using proper local anaesthesia for procedures that require it?

If the answer to any of these is no, walk out. The risk to your health — and the cost of fixing complications — far outweighs the short-term saving.
